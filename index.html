I'm a Film and Media student at Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU), with a deep curiosity for how moving image and sound come together to shape emotion and meaning.

My passion lives at a very particular intersection: Korean variety shows. What draws me isn't just the entertainment — it's the craft. The way subtitle effects amplify a punchline, how editing rhythm accelerates tension, and how cast chemistry creates moments that resonate long after an episode ends.

I want to unpack these mechanics using AI and data visualization — building tools that surface the patterns behind what makes audiences laugh, react, and come back.

Korean Variety Show Trend Dashboard

A creative data visualization dashboard that analyzes the patterns and trends in Korean variety shows — examining how humor lands, how editing styles evolve across programs, how subtitle text effects have changed over the years, and how cast dynamics correlate with viewership and audience sentiment.

By combining AI-driven analysis with interactive charts, the project aims to make the invisible mechanics of variety production visible and explorable.
